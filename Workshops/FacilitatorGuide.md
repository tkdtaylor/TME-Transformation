# Business Readiness Assessment Facilitator Guide

> Companion to [BusinessReadinessAssessment.md](BusinessReadinessAssessment.md). This guide helps a facilitator run the AIAF-grounded workshop without expanding it into a general TAF assessment.

## Purpose

The workshop creates the starting baseline for AI adoption: business drivers, capability map, data readiness, shadow AI inventory, AI disruption analysis, AIAF maturity score, and a prioritized action backlog. The facilitator's job is to keep the group honest, specific, and evidence-oriented.

## Pre-Workshop Preparation

| Task | Owner | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Confirm executive sponsor | Facilitator / client lead | Sponsor should open the workshop and reinforce that honest gaps are useful |
| Confirm participants | Facilitator | Include business, technology, data, security/risk, HR, and legal/compliance voices |
| Gather existing artifacts | Client lead | Strategy docs, org chart, system inventory, data classification policy, AI policy if any |
| Send pre-read | Facilitator | AIAF overview, workshop agenda, definitions of capability / thin work / valued work |
| Choose working format | Facilitator | Whiteboard, spreadsheet, shared doc, Miro, Notion, or equivalent |
| Define decision rules | Sponsor | Clarify who resolves disagreements and what decisions can be made in-session |

## Recommended Agenda

| Segment | Time | Output |
|---|---:|---|
| Opening and goals | 15 min | Scope, ground rules, desired decisions |
| Module 1: Business context | 30-45 min | Strategic drivers and constraints |
| Module 2: Capability inventory | 90 min | Capability list, strategic importance, concentration risks |
| Module 3: Data inventory | 60-90 min | Data classes, AI readiness, knowledge asset gaps |
| Module 4: Technology and shadow AI | 45-60 min | Current systems, shadow AI, build/buy candidates |
| Module 5: Wardley map | 60-90 min | Capability map and disruption markers |
| Module 6: AI disruption analysis | 60-90 min | Threats, opportunities, workforce impacts |
| Module 7: Maturity scoring | 45-60 min | AIAF maturity baseline and readiness checklist |
| Module 8: Synthesis | 30-45 min | Starting profile, decision register, action backlog |

For a two-half-day format, run Modules 1-4 in the first session and Modules 5-8 in the second. For an eight-week sprint, keep each module to one focused working session and carry forward open questions between sessions.

## Facilitation Principles

- Keep the workshop grounded in AIAF outcomes: AI readiness, AI governance, AI risk, and AI adoption sequencing.
- Use TAF only as context when platform, security, operations, or people maturity affects AI adoption.
- Treat disagreement as data. Capture who disagrees, why, and what evidence would resolve it.
- Prefer approximate but honest answers over polished answers that hide uncertainty.
- Separate facts, assumptions, and decisions. Do not let assumptions quietly become decisions.
- Keep risk tiering conservative until evidence supports a lower tier.
- Make ownership explicit. A gap without an owner is only an observation.

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like | Facilitator Response |
|---|---|---|
| Tool-first discussion | Participants debate products before defining business outcomes | Return to strategic drivers and use case value |
| Overbroad AI ambition | The group wants an agent before documenting the workflow | Require SOP, decision points, exceptions, and handoffs |
| Data optimism | Teams assume data is usable because it exists somewhere | Ask for owner, classification, quality, access, and retention evidence |
| Shadow AI defensiveness | Participants hide unsanctioned usage | Reframe shadow AI as unmet demand and risk visibility |
| False consensus | Everyone nods, but owners disagree after the session | Ask each accountable owner to state agreement or concern explicitly |
| Governance theater | Policies exist but no technical control or evidence exists | Ask how the control is enforced and how it is audited |
| Pilot enthusiasm | A pilot looks successful but has no production owner or budget | Apply the Pilot-to-Production Gate early |

## Required Outputs

By the end of the workshop, the group should have:

- Strategic drivers and constraints
- Business capability inventory
- Data and AI knowledge asset inventory
- Shadow AI inventory
- Wardley map with AI disruption markers
- Thin work / valued work classification
- AI disruption priority matrix
- Initial AI Decision Register
- AIAF maturity baseline and readiness checklist score
- 0-90 day and 12-month action backlog

## After the Workshop

| Timing | Action |
|---|---|
| Within 2 business days | Send captured outputs, unresolved questions, and decision log |
| Within 1 week | Confirm owners for immediate actions |
| Within 30 days | Select candidate pilots and complete use case cards |
| Within 90 days | Re-run readiness checklist, shadow AI audit, and action backlog review |

## Conversion Note

Workshop outputs can live in whatever format the organization actually uses: documents, spreadsheets, whiteboards, tickets, GRC systems, or portfolio tools. Preserve the ownership, evidence, review cadence, and decision history fields when converting from the source templates.
